\section{Exodus 21}\label{Exodus 21}
\heading{The Lord's law of servants, marriage, and the death penalty~--- eye for an eye, tooth for a tooth~--- damage caused by oxen}
\begin{enumerate}[align=center]
    \verse{Exodus^21:1} These are the judgments\footnote{laws} that you shall set before them.\footnote{Lit., ``their face''}%
    \verse{Exodus^21:2} If you acquire a Hebrew slave\footnote{male slave, servant. This person is subject to Jewish jurisprudence as evidenced by the use of the word \Hebrew{עבד}} six years, he shall serve thee; in the seventh year he shall go free without having to pay.\footnote{Like indentured servanthood.}%
    \verse{Exodus^21:3} If he comes alone, alone shall he go out. If he is married,\footnote{The husband/lord of a woman.} his wife shall go with him.%
    \verse{Exodus^21:4} If his master giveth him a wife and she bears sons or daughters to him, their\footnote{her} offspring\footnote{children} shall belong to the master and the man shall go forth by himself.%

    \verse{Exodus^21:15} He who strikes his father or his mother\footnote{\dots he\dots} shall surely be put to death.%
    \verse{Exodus^21:16} Anyone who kidnaps\footnote{steals} someone\footnote{to sell them} and he is caught with the man still in his possession:\footnote{power} he shall surely be put to death.\footnote{In other words, kidnapping is a capital offense.}%
    \verse{Exodus^21:17} He who curses\footnote{reviles, insults~--- but harsher than we tend to think} his father or his mother shall sure be put to death.%
    \verse{Exodus^21:18} And if men quarrel\footnote{fight} and one man hits another (whether with a stone or his fists), but not to kill him~--- merely to put him to bed~---%
    \verse{Exodus^21:19} if the person doesn't die, but is confined to bed XXXX he will pay for his lost time and see that he is healed.%
    \verse{Exodus^21:20} If a man strikes\footnote{hits} his male or female servant with a staff\footnote{rod} and that person dies under his hand then he will surely be punished in proxy for him.\footnote{or her.}%
    \verse{Exodus^21:21} But if a day or two goes by and he\footnote{i.e., the slave} gets up, he\footnote{the master} will not be punished for it is his\footnote{Understood: own.} property.%
    \verse{Exodus^21:22} If men fight and strike a pregnant woman so that she has a miscarriage,\footnote{Lit., ``her children go forth.''} but there is no further harm, he will surely be fined according to what the husband deems right, and he\footnote{the other man} will give according to what is assessed.%
    \verse{Exodus^21:23} If there is a serious injury or death, you shall take life in place of\footnote{for} life:%
    \verse{Exodus^21:24} an eye for an eye, a tooth for a tooth, a hand for a hand, a foot for a foot,\footnote{This is significantly more merciful than people let on. In other ancient civilizations (e.g., Hammurabi's code) there were different punishments depending on the person's social status.}%
    \verse{Exodus^21:25} burning for burning, wound for wound, strike for strike.%
    \verse{Exodus^21:26} If a man strikes the eye of his male or female servant and knocks it out,\footnote{Lit., destroys it} he will let him go free in penance for his eye.%
    \verse{Exodus^21:27} If the tooth of a male or female servant is knocked out, he must let them go free for\footnote{\`a cause de} their tooth.%
    \verse{Exodus^21:28} If an ox\footnote{``Bull'' can be supplied for every following instance of ``ox'' in this chapter.} gores a man or a woman and that person dies, the ox will surely be stoned to death, his flesh shall not be eaten, and the owner shall not be liable.%
    \verse{Exodus^21:29} If the ox has been known to gore in the past and this was known\footnote{made known} to its master(s), and his master didn't keep him under guard, and it kills a man or a woman, the ox will be stoned and his master will be put to death as well.%
    \verse{Exodus^21:30} If a payment is laid upon him, he shall pay\footnote{give} a redemption\footnote{ransom} for his life according to everything that is laid upon him.%
